Egusi Soup

Egusi Soup is a rich and hearty West African soup made with ground melon seeds, leafy vegetables, and assorted meats or fish. Widely enjoyed across Nigeria, this flavorful soup is known for its thick texture and nutty taste. Often cooked with palm oil, stockfish, crayfish, and seasoning, Egusi is typically served with pounded yam, eba, or fufu, making it a staple in Nigerian homes and festive occasions.

Instruction section

  1. Wash the meat and stockfish thoroughly, then boil them in a pot with water, adding chopped onions, salt, and seasoning cubes. Cook until tender.
  2. In a separate pot, heat the palm oil until it is hot but not smoking. Add the ground egusi and stir-fry for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the cooked meat and stockfish to the egusi mixture, stirring well to combine.
  4. Pour in some of the meat stock (the water from boiling the meat) to achieve your desired soup consistency.
  5. Add the sliced vegetables and allow to simmer for another 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Adjust seasoning if necessary, then serve hot with pounded yam, eba, or fufu.

Additional Information

For a richer, more aromatic flavor, consider lightly toasting the egusi seeds before grinding them. If you enjoy a bit of heat and depth, blend in some fresh peppers (like scotch bonnet, bell pepper, and onion) and add them during the frying stage. When frying the egusi paste, avoid constant stirringâ€”let it cook undisturbed for a few minutes to develop a desirable, chunky texture. Soaking stockfish in warm water beforehand helps soften it and remove any grit or excess salt. Be sure to use high-quality red palm oil, as it significantly enhances the taste and color of the soup. Finally, the type of vegetable you use affects the flavor: bitterleaf adds a traditional, slightly bitter edge, while spinach or ugu offers a milder, more neutral taste.
